This practice is called the 4-6 breath. It's one of the most well-researched tools for shifting the nervous system out of a sympathetic (threat) state and into a parasympathetic (recovery) state. You can do it in five minutes, anywhere, without anyone knowing you're doing it.

Here's how it works:

Today's Practice — The 4-6 Breath Reset
1
Sit upright with both feet flat on the floor. No need to close your eyes — you can do this at your desk between meetings.
2
Breathe in through your nose for a count of 4. Slow and steady — let the belly expand first, then the chest.
3
Breathe out through your mouth for a count of 6. The exhale is longer than the inhale — this is what activates the vagus nerve and slows the heart rate.
4
Repeat for 5 minutes — or as few as 6 cycles if time is short. Don't force it. Find the rhythm that feels natural.

After you finish, check in with the area of your body you noticed yesterday. Notice if anything has shifted — even slightly.

The key insight here is that the exhale activates the parasympathetic nervous system. When you extend the exhale beyond the inhale, you're directly communicating to your nervous system that the threat has passed. You're not thinking your way out of stress — you're physiologically shifting out of it.

Use this before difficult conversations, after a hard call, or whenever you notice the pressure building. It doesn't require belief or practice time — it works because of how the body is wired.
