Welcome to Day 1. I'm glad you're here.

Most people who carry chronic pressure have been carrying it for so long they've stopped noticing it. It becomes background noise — a low hum of urgency, tightness, or vigilance that feels like just how life is. Today's practice is about making that invisible thing visible.

It takes about three minutes. You can do it right now, or at any quiet moment today — before a meeting, after lunch, or just before bed.

Today's Practice — The Pressure Map

Find somewhere to sit without your phone in your hand. Close your eyes or soften your gaze downward. Take two slow breaths — just to arrive.

Then ask yourself: Where in my body do I feel the pressure right now?

Don't analyze it. Just notice. Is it in your chest? Your jaw? Your shoulders? Your stomach? Somewhere else? Scan slowly from your head downward and notice where you feel tightness, heaviness, or holding.

When you find it, just acknowledge it. Place a hand there if that feels natural. You don't need to fix it. You're just making contact with something that's been trying to get your attention.

Stay with it for 60 seconds. Then breathe out slowly and open your eyes.

That's it. You've done Day 1.

This practice matters because most stress-management approaches try to change what you're thinking. This works differently — it starts with the body, where pressure actually lives. The act of noticing without trying to fix is itself a regulation signal. Your nervous system responds to it.
